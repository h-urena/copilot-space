# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Product Owner

### Role Summary
Product Owners drive the product vision and prioritize the backlog to maximize business value. They serve as the bridge between stakeholders and the delivery team, ensuring that development efforts align with customer needs and business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Define and communicate the product vision and strategy
- Maintain and prioritize the product backlog
- Write clear user stories with acceptance criteria
- Make prioritization decisions balancing business value and technical constraints
- Collaborate with stakeholders to gather requirements and validate solutions
- Accept or reject completed work based on acceptance criteria

### Goals
- Maximize return on investment for development efforts
- Ensure the product delivers measurable business value
- Maintain a clear, well-prioritized backlog
- Foster strong collaboration between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Daily interaction with Developers during standups and story refinement
- Regular sync with Product Managers on strategic direction and roadmap
- Backlog refinement sessions with the delivery team
- Sprint planning and review meetings with all stakeholders
- Collaborate with Project Managers on release planning and dependencies

---

## Quality Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Leads own the test strategy, ensure release criteria are met, and coordinate all quality assurance activities. They establish quality standards and work with the team to build quality into every stage of development.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the overall test strategy and quality standards
- Own the test plan and ensure adequate test coverage
- Coordinate quality assurance activities across the team
- Establish and validate release criteria
- Identify quality risks and recommend mitigations
- Lead test automation initiatives and best practices
- Monitor quality metrics and trends

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality releases that meet customer expectations
- Minimize production defects and rework
- Build quality awareness and practices into the development process
- Maintain comprehensive test coverage and documentation

### Typical Communication
- Daily collaboration with Developers on testing approaches and results
- Regular updates to Product Owners and Product Managers on quality status
- Test plan reviews and quality gate discussions with Project Managers
- Quality metrics reporting to stakeholders
- Coordinate with Release Managers on release readiness assessments

---

## Stakeholder Representative

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Representatives serve as the primary point of contact for business needs and feedback. They ensure that the delivery aligns with business goals and represent the interests of various stakeholders throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Represent business interests and requirements to the delivery team
- Clarify business needs and validate that deliverables meet expectations
- Provide timely feedback on proposed solutions and prototypes
- Escalate business concerns and decisions to appropriate leadership
- Participate in requirements gathering and acceptance reviews
- Communicate project status and impacts to business stakeholders

### Goals
- Ensure deliverables align with business objectives and strategy
- Facilitate clear communication between business and technical teams
- Minimize rework by providing early and clear requirements
- Build stakeholder confidence in delivery outcomes

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops with Product Owners and Product Managers
- Sprint reviews and demonstrations to validate features
- Regular status updates from Project Managers
- Feedback sessions with Developers on prototypes and implementations
- Business impact discussions with leadership and project teams

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate all aspects of software releases, from planning through deployment and post-release validation. They ensure smooth, controlled releases by managing dependencies, schedules, and communication across all involved parties.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and coordinate release schedules and activities
- Manage release dependencies and integration points
- Create and maintain release documentation and runbooks
- Coordinate go-live activities and deployment execution
- Oversee post-release validation and monitoring
- Manage release risks and rollback procedures
- Ensure compliance with release policies and governance

### Goals
- Execute successful, zero-downtime releases
- Minimize release-related incidents and rollbacks
- Maintain clear release schedules and stakeholder alignment
- Continuously improve release processes and automation

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings with Project Managers and Developers
- Quality gate reviews with Quality Leads before each release
- Deployment coordination with technical teams
- Release status updates to Product Managers and stakeholders
- Post-release reviews with the entire delivery team
- Stakeholder notifications through formal release communications

---

## Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Agile Coaches guide teams on Agile methodology best practices and facilitate continuous improvement. They help teams adopt and refine Agile practices, remove impediments to effectiveness, and foster a culture of collaboration and learning.

### Responsibilities
- Coach teams and leadership on Agile principles and practices
- Facilitate Agile ceremonies (standups, retrospectives, planning)
- Identify and help remove team impediments and blockers
- Guide teams through process improvements and adaptations
- Mentor Project Managers and team leads on servant leadership
- Foster psychological safety and team collaboration
- Track and improve team health and effectiveness metrics

### Goals
- Build high-performing, self-organizing teams
- Drive continuous improvement in team practices and outcomes
- Increase team agility and adaptability to change
- Create a sustainable pace and healthy team culture

### Typical Communication
- Daily observation and coaching of team interactions
- One-on-one coaching sessions with Developers, Product Owners, and Project Managers
- Retrospective facilitation with the entire team
- Process improvement workshops and training sessions
- Team health assessments and feedback to leadership
- Collaborate with Product Managers and Project Managers on team structure and workflows
